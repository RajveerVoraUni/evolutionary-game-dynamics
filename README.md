## Deterministic and Stochastic Replicator Dynamics: Stability, Cycles, and Strategic Evolution

#### ABSTRACT:
We combine analytical stability theory, stochastic perturbation analysis, and a financial market application to demonstrate how replicator dynamics unifies equilibrium selection, cyclic coexistence, and regime transition under strategic adaptation. Specifically, we develop and analyse a simulation framework covering four canonical regimes: the Prisoner’s Dilemma, Hawk–Dove, Rock–Paper–Scissors (RPS), and a market microstructure game. For each deterministic system, fixed-point behaviour is characterised through linearisation and eigenvalue classification of the Jacobian. The zero-sum RPS game admits a conserved quantity H(x) = x1x2x3, verified numerically to within 0.01% drift. Stochastic extensions via Euler–Maruyama integration show that even weak noise destroys invariant level sets and induces diffusive boundary exploration absent from the deterministic system. In the market microstructure application, replicator dynamics illustrates how mean-reversion can dominate under crowding costs, and a sensitivity analysis of the momentum self-payoff identifies a sharp regime transition separating momentum-dominated from mean-reversion-dominated equilibria.
